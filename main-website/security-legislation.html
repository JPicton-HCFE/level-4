    Features

    There are seven main principles for the DPA that organisations must adhere to when operating within the UK.

    1. Lawfulness, Fairness, and Transparency - This requires organisations to process data within a way that is lawful, fair, and transparent. They need to clearly state and provide the user the information on how their data will be used.

    2. Purpose Limitation - This asks organisations to collect data for specified, explicit or legitimate purposes and not to further process this data in an incompatible way with those purposes.

    3. Minimisation - This is to ensure that organisations only collect data relevant to the specified intended purpose, or at least should be.

    4. Accuracy - Organisations should have the most up to date information/data otherwise the outdated data should be rectified or deleted.

    5. Storage Limitation - When organisations collect data, they should hold that data for no longer than it's intended use. They should have clear retention policies, whilst also dispose of any data that is no longer redeemed necessary.

    6. Integrity and Confidentiality - With the collected data, organisations must process the personal data within a secure manner. They must protect against accidental loss, destruction or damage to the data. Some security measures they could use, include encryption and anonymisation.

    7. Accountability - When handling the data, the data controller (the person who determines the purpose(s) and means of processing of personal data), must take responsibility in how they handle personal data. They must also be able to demonstrate compliance with the DPA (2018).
